Howard Hughes

The real Iron Man!

Howard Robard Hughes Jr., was born in Harris County, Texas in the 24 of December 1905, son of Howard Robard Hughes Sr., a successfol inventor and businessman from Missouri and Allene Stone Gano member of one of the most proeminent families in Texas. At a young age Howard shows to be very interested and had great aptitude for science, technology and engineering, at age 11 he built the first wireless radio transmitter in Houston and at 12 a motorized bycicle, with parts from his father steam engine. At age 14 he came in touch with the biggest passion of his life, aviation, when he convinced his father to buy him a flight lesson, it will be a connection for all his life, ending only with his death in 1976.

In 1922 the first stroke in his life, his mother dies suddenly, and then 2 years later his father also from an heart attack, who puts the young Howard(19 at the time) the major heir of Hughes Tool Company, probably the biggest manufacturer of drill bits for oil prospection in the world at the time. This events turn the young Howard in a very healthy man, and was the beginning of a fantastic life trip with business interests in entertainement, real estate, aviation and aeroespace, who turn Howard in to a billionaire and one of most proeminent and eccentric figures of the 20st century.

Personal Life

A complete womanizer, married twice, first with Ellen Rice in 1925(divorced in 1929) and with actress Jean Peters in 1957(divorced in 1971) Howard dated some of the main female stars of Hollywood of his time, Billie Dove, Katharine Hepburn, Faith Domergue, Bette Davis, Ava Gardner, Olivia de Havilland, Hedy Lamarr, Ginger Rogers, Janet Leigh, Rita Hayworth, Mamie Van Doren and Gene Terney.

Eccentric and reclusive

Hughes always denote an eccentric lifestyle, he also suffered from Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder(OCD), since young age he demonstrate a severe germ phobia, who led to during his later days, his aides only contact with him if they weare white gloves and use tissues to pick objects to him.

In 1958 after his second mental breakdown, Hughes tell his aides that he want to go to an studio near his house to watch movies and not to be disturbed until he say so, he ending been inside f the studio for 4 straight months only eating chocolate bars, chicken and drinking milk, surrounded of kleenex boxes that he compulsive arranged and stacked, often naked in is chair, when he finally came out his hygiene was terrible since in all this time he didn't bathed cut his air or nails. His eating habits were also peculiar, he was capable of eating the same thing for long periods of time and had a rare fixation with peas, he only eat the small ones and putt the larger aside.

Business

Entertainment

After inherited the empire and fortune left by his parents the main goal of is life was to create is own mark on the world for not to be seen only as an healthy playboy that got fortune on the expense of the achivements of his progenitor.

His first decision was to go to Hollywood with his newly wife and start a career as movie producer, he got the funds and he know ery well the enviroment by influence of his paternal uncle, the famous novelist, screenwriter and film-director Rupert Hughes.His first project Swell Hogan was a financial disaster, but the two that followed, Everibody's Acting(1926) and Two Arabian Knights(1927) were comercial successes,with the last one wining an Oscar. But he made his mark in the industry with Hells Angels in 1930, an epic aviation war film with First World War as scenario. The movie even today is defined as one of the best and with most realistic scenes of air combat.The production was an astonishing $2.5 million at the time, the movie also turn momentaneous Howard as the owner of the biggest private air force in the world, with almost 90 planes. In the movie more than 100 pilots were used to record the figthing scenes.This will be awake up call for Hughes to pursued is big passion, aviation as a business, making him from 1950 until his death the richest American in the world and one of the first billionaires.

Hughes will make his mark in the Entertainment business with two more remarkable movies, first Scarface in 1932 and The Outlaw in 1946 the movie that launch Jane Russel career.

The decreasing interest of Hughes in the business not stopping him to adquire a majority stake in the famous studios RKO in 1948, the studios once one of the biggest in Hollywood, were facing losses for several years, and the entrance of Hughes, seen as King Midas for his achivements as an businessman, only deteriorate the state of the studios, at this time Hughes health starts to getting worst everiday, and in 1955 finally he gave up of the studios, walking out with a $10 million in profit overall after selling it to General Tire and Rubber Company.

Aviation and Airlines

Since young, aviation was his biggest passion, Hughes was a skilled pilot, with enumorous airplanes for his private use, while pursuing his entertainment businesses he set various records in aviation, as the airspeed record in 1935 with his H1-Racer, the first airplane buit by his Hughes Aircraft, a company created only with the proposal of building airplanes for his private use, and in 1938 the round-the-world flight record in just 91 hours.

This achievements led Hughes to be considered an American hero. Also he start to realize how the airline business will get a boom in the next years due to the evolution of the aviation technology, so in 1939 he start to buy interest in TWA, a strugling Californian airline, by 1944 he have full control of the company that he will transfom it in an transcontinental airline(by this time only Pan Am got that statue).

At the same time his Hughes Aircaft start to work for the goverment as one of the new defense contractors during the WWII, first they developed the Hughes D-2, that was conceived as a five crew member bomber, but the militaries don't aproved the airplane, and instead ordered 100 of a reconaissance development airplane of the D-2, and that was the controversial and near fatal XF-11. The date July 7,1946, was an important date in Hughes life, because it was in that day that he crashed the XF-11 during an flight test and nearly die, the physical and psicological consequences of this accident, was considered by a lot as the trigger to the decreasing of physical an mental sanity of Hughes due to his new dependency on painkillers and antidepressives medications.

But the biggest, more complex and known project was the construction of the flying boat, known as the H-4 Hercules or Spruce Goose for is critics, it was the largest aircraft made of wood(Duramold process), with the biggest wingspan at 319 feet and 11 inches and also the most heavies aircraft ever built(this records only got broken more than 40 years later), the plane only flew once for one mile and 70 feet above the water in Long Beach harbour on November 2,1947 with Hughes at the commands, it was a common practice for Hughes to test his planes. After this flight the Hercules stayed at the Long Beach harbour and Hughes ordered to be built an enormous climate-controlled hangar around it, with a full-time crew of 300 workers only to maintain the plane in flight conditions, in 1962 the crew was reduced to nearly 50 persons.After the death of Hughes in 1976 the crew was disbanded and the plane was finally sold in 1980 to a private company.The plane stay today in display at the Evergreen Aviation & Space Museum in Mcinville, Oregon.

Howard Hughes Medical Institute

Created in 1953, initially located in Miami, Florida(currently in Chevy Chase, Maryland), is currently today the fifth wealthiest charitable foundation in the world and the number one in biological and medical research with $23.8 billion in endowment.

It was created with the goal of biomedical research after Hughes first will(not really valid, as we going to see ahead) in 1925 stipulated that is estate will be used to create a medical institute, but the real initial purpose of the institute was a way of Hughes avoid IRS taxes, so Hughes gave all his stock in the Hughes Aircraft Company to the institute, thereby turning the aerospace and defense contractor into a for-profit entity of a fully tax-exempt charity. Hughes' internist, Verne Mason, who treated Hughes after his 1946 aircraft crash, was chairman of the institute's medical advisory committee. The Howard Hughes Medical Institute's new board of trustees sold Hughes Aircraft in 1985 to General Motors for $5.2 billion, allowing the institute to grow dramatically.

In 1954, Hughes transferred Hughes Aircraft to the foundation, which paid Hughes Tool Co. $18,000,000 for the assets. The foundation leased the land from Hughes Tool Co., which then subleased it to Hughes Aircraft Corp. The difference in rent, $2,000,000 per year, became the foundation's working capital.

The deal was the topic of a protracted legal battle between Hughes and the Internal Revenue Service, which Hughes ultimately won. After his death in 1976, many thought that the balance of Hughes' estate would go to the institute, although it was ultimately divided among his cousins and other heirs, given the lack of a will to the contrary.

Death

On April 5,1976 at 1.27pm Hughes die on board of an aircraft that was bringing him to the Methodist Hospital in Houston.

His reclusiveness and possibly his drug use made him practically unrecognizable. His hair, beard, fingernails, and toenails were long, his tall 6 ft 4 in (193 cm) frame now weighed barely 90 pounds (41 kg), and the FBI had to use fingerprints to conclusively identify the body. Howard Hughes' alias, John T. Conover, was used when his body arrived at a morgue in Houston on the day of his death.

A subsequent autopsy recorded kidney failure as the cause of death. Hughes was in extremely poor physical condition at the time of his death. He suffered from malnutrition and was covered in bedsores. While his kidneys were damaged, his other internal organs, including his brain, which had no visible damage other than illnesses, were deemed perfectly healthy. X-rays revealed five broken-off hypodermic needles in the flesh of his arms. To inject codeine into his muscles, Hughes had used glass syringes with metal needles that easily became detached.

Hughes is buried next to his parents at Glenwood Cemetery in Houston.

Finally in 1983 his $2.5 billion estate, was split among 22 cousins.
